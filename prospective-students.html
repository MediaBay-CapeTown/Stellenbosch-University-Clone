What is Computer Science?

Computer science is the theory and practice of algorithms. It is a wonderful combination of art (invention and design), science (analysis and experimentation), and engineering to solve problems with computers. Our students learn to program, develop, and manage large software systems in a team, but – most importantly – to frame problems in terms of computational processes.

What is the Course All About?

Computer Science deals with computer systems, both the hardware and the software. We take a scientific approach to the subject, emphasizing the principles of computer systems instead of the nitty-gritty.

First Year

The focus is on learning to program using Java language, providing an excellent introduction to other languages like C++ and C.

Second Year

Focus on data structures and algorithms, and computer architecture with low-level programming.

Third Year

Core modules in concurrent programming, databases, web-centric computing, computer networks, and software engineering. Electives in Machine Learning, Automata theory, and Computer Vision.

Related Questions

Is Computer Science the same as Computer Literacy, Information Science, or Scientific Computing?

No, they are different:

  • Computer Literacy: Basic tools (word processors, spreadsheets, scientific tools)
  • Scientific Computing: Tools like LaTeX and Python for technical documentation and numerical computing
  • Information Science: Dynamic interplay between IT and organizational/societal contexts
  • Computer Science: Algorithms, software development, and computational problem-solving

Should I Study Computer Science or Electronic Engineering?

Both are excellent choices in high demand. Electronic Engineering focuses on hardware design and software for engineering applications. Computer Science specializes in software and algorithms. Choose based on your primary interest: engineering and hardware (Electronic Engineering) or software solutions (Computer Science). We recommend speaking with staff from both departments.
