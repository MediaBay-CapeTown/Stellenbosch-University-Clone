Master's Degree Programmes

Advanced postgraduate studies for career advancement and specialization

Why Study a Master's at Stellenbosch?

Our Master's programmes are internationally recognized qualifications that deepen your expertise, enhance career prospects, and prepare you for leadership roles. Study under world-class researchers in state-of-the-art facilities.

Programme Options

  • • By Coursework (1-2 years) - Structured modules + research component
  • • By Thesis (2 years) - Research-focused with limited coursework
  • • Accelerated (1 year) - Intensive full-time programmes
  • • Professional (2 years) - Part-time evening/weekend study
  • • Online Blended - Flexible learning with residential modules

Entry Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in related field (min. 60%)
  • English language proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS)
  • Statement of purpose (1-2 pages)
  • Academic references (2-3)

Masters in Computer Science

A two-year research-based Master's degree focused on independent thesis work and advanced research training.

Our Masters degree is a two-year programme based on thesis work. The goal is to teach students how to evaluate and conduct world-class research, and become a master in their field. While the work is not required to be original cutting-edge research, it often is. The skills acquired are much sought-after in industry and academia.

You need the agreement of a staff member who will supervise your Masters thesis. Browse our Research pages and staff publications to identify potential supervisors and topics. It is important to obtain a supervisor's agreement before applying when possible.

Admission requires an Honours degree in Computer Science or an approved equivalent qualification. Additional background or supplementary studies may be required as determined by your supervisor(s).

Applying & Supervision

Admission is by selection. You must arrange agreement with a supervising staff member before applying. Prepare a clear research proposal and contact potential supervisors to discuss suitability.

  • Agree on a supervisor before application where possible
  • Submit transcripts, CV, research proposal and references
  • English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS) for international applicants

Career & Outcomes

Most Masters graduates move into advanced research roles or industry positions that require strong analytical, writing, and problem-solving skills. Those who continue to PhD programmes go on to academic and research careers.

For more details, see our postgraduate pages and contact postgrad@cs.sun.ac.za.
