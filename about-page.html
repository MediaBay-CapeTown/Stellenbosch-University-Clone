Who We Are

Department Overview

The Department of Computer Science & Informatics at Stellenbosch University is one of the foremost centres of computing excellence on the African continent. Established in 1972, we have grown from a small unit within the Faculty of Science into a thriving academic department with over 1,800 students and 60 academic staff members.

Our department offers a comprehensive suite of programmes spanning undergraduate degrees, honours, master's, and doctoral qualifications. We are committed to producing graduates who are not only technically proficient but also ethically grounded and socially aware — ready to lead Africa's digital transformation.

We collaborate extensively with industry partners, government bodies, and international universities, ensuring our research and teaching remain at the cutting edge of global developments in computing, artificial intelligence, data science, and software engineering.

In 2026 we launched the African AI Research Hub, secured a #1 ranking among African CS departments, and unveiled a ten‑year strategic plan focusing on sustainable growth and global engagement.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To advance knowledge in computer science and informatics through world-class research and teaching, while nurturing graduates who are equipped to solve Africa's most pressing challenges through technology and innovation.

  • Deliver excellent, relevant, and accessible education
  • Conduct impactful research with real-world applications
  • Foster inclusive and transformative academic community
  • Engage meaningfully with industry and society

Our Vision

To be the leading department of computer science on the African continent — recognised globally for research excellence, innovative teaching, and our transformative impact on society through technology.

By 2030

Rank among the top 150 computer science departments globally in the QS World University Rankings.

Strategic Focus

Lead Africa's AI and data science revolution through research, education, and industry partnerships.

Our Journey

Department History

Over five decades of academic excellence, innovation, and growth in computing education and research.

72
1972 — Founded

Department Established

The Department of Computer Science was established within the Faculty of Science with 3 founding staff members and 45 students, offering the first computing degree in the Western Cape.

89
1989 — Expansion

First Research Centre Launched

The Centre for High Performance Computing was established, marking the department's commitment to research excellence and attracting its first international research grants.

03
2003 — Milestone

Informatics Programme Added

The department expanded to include Informatics, reflecting the growing importance of information systems and human-computer interaction in the digital economy.

24
2024 — Present

AI & Data Science Hub

Launch of the African AI Research Hub, a R120M initiative positioning SU as the continent's leading centre for artificial intelligence research and education.

26
2026

Strategic Plan 2026-2027

Launch of our ten‑point strategic plan focusing on sustainable growth, global impact, and deepening community engagement.

Welcome to the Department of Computer Science & Informatics at Stellenbosch University. We stand at a remarkable moment in history — a time when computing and artificial intelligence are reshaping every aspect of human life, and when Africa has an unprecedented opportunity to lead rather than follow.

Our department has always been driven by a simple but powerful conviction: that great computing education, combined with rigorous research and a deep commitment to social impact, can transform lives and communities. Over the past five decades, we have built a department that is not only technically excellent but also deeply rooted in the African context.

Whether you are a prospective student, a researcher, an industry partner, or a member of our community, I invite you to explore what we have to offer. Together, we are building the digital future of Africa — and we would love for you to be part of that journey.

Prof. David Nkosi
Head of Department, Computer Science & Informatics
