Advanced Thermodynamics II

Module Code: PHY 310 | Faculty: Science | Level: 3

Module Overview

This advanced module builds on fundamental thermodynamic principles to explore complex systems, phase transitions, and statistical mechanics. Students will develop critical understanding of heat transfer mechanisms, entropy, and practical applications in engineering and physical sciences.

Key Information

  • • Semester: 2nd Semester
  • • Credits: 12 (ECTS)
  • • Contact Hours: 36 hours (lectures + tutorials)
  • • Self-Study: 60+ hours
  • • Lecturer: Prof. David Koelpin
  • • Office: Science Building Room 304

Prerequisites

Students must have successfully completed:

  • ✓ Physics 201 (Thermodynamics I)
  • ✓ Mathematics 211 (Calculus)
  • ✓ Physics 211 (Classical Mechanics)

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  • 1 Understand and apply the laws of thermodynamics to complex systems
  • 2 Analyze phase transitions and critical phenomena using statistical mechanics
  • 3 Calculate thermodynamic properties of real gases and liquids
  • 4 Design experiments and solve advanced thermodynamics problems
  • 5 Apply theoretical knowledge to engineering applications

Assessment Methods

  • Continuous Assessment (Assignments) 20%
  • Tutorial Participation 10%
  • Laboratory Practicals 20%
  • Final Examination (3 hours) 50%

Module Content

  • • Foundations of Statistical Mechanics
  • • Boltzmann Distribution and Entropy
  • • Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena
  • • Real Gases and Van der Waals Equation
  • • Heat Transfer and Conduction
  • • Applications in Engineering Systems

Required Materials

Textbook: "Statistical Thermodynamics" by D.A. McQuarrie (3rd Edition)

Additional Resources: Laboratory manual provided; access to thermodynamics simulation software; online portal for assignment submission
