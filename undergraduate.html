Programme Overview

BSc Computer Science

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Stellenbosch University is a rigorous, three-year degree that equips students with deep theoretical foundations and practical skills in computing. Graduates are prepared for careers in software engineering, data science, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and academic research.

Our curriculum is regularly updated in consultation with industry partners to ensure graduates meet the demands of a rapidly evolving technology landscape. Students benefit from state-of-the-art computing labs, mentorship from world-class faculty, and access to a vibrant research community.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our undergraduate programmes.

The minimum APS score for BSc Computer Science is 34 points (excluding Life Orientation). Additionally, you must achieve at least 70% (Level 6) in Mathematics and 60% (Level 5) in Physical Sciences. Meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee admission, as places are competitive and allocated based on academic merit.

Yes, it is possible to change your degree programme, but this is subject to availability of places and academic performance. Changes can typically be made at the end of your first year. You will need to apply formally through the Faculty of Science office. Some modules may be credited towards your new programme, but this is assessed on a case-by-case basis.

The BSc Computer Science is currently offered as a full-time, contact programme only. There is no part-time or distance learning option at the undergraduate level. However, some postgraduate programmes and short courses are available in blended or online formats. Please contact the department for more information about flexible study options.

Several financial aid options are available: NSFAS funding for qualifying South African students, SU Merit Bursaries for top-performing applicants, departmental scholarships for students in financial need with strong academic records, and industry-sponsored bursaries from partners such as Google, Microsoft, and local tech companies. Applications for most bursaries open simultaneously with programme applications.

Students are exposed to a range of programming languages throughout the degree. Year 1 begins with Python for introductory programming. Year 2 introduces Java for object-oriented programming and C/C++ for systems programming. Year 3 modules may involve R, SQL, JavaScript, and specialised languages depending on elective choices. The focus is on learning programming concepts that transfer across languages, rather than mastery of a single language.

Yes, the BSc Computer Science is an excellent foundation for postgraduate study. Students with a strong academic record (typically 65%+ average) are eligible to apply for the BSc Honours in Computer Science, which is a one-year research-focused degree. From Honours, students can progress to an MSc and ultimately a PhD. The department actively encourages talented undergraduates to pursue research careers.
